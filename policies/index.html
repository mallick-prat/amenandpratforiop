A Real, Reformative, Revolutionary
Vision for OUR IOP 💖

With three years of leadership experience across eight programs, we’ve been blessed by the opportunity to learn from and with the IOP community. Our vision is about rebuilding the IOP’s past to streamline its present and reimagine its future, all with the hope of building an IOP that creates the most revolutionary servants and leaders.
Join us as we remember, revive, and reimagine our IOP!

Remember - Recognizing Our Past

The IOP has been around a while and over 50 years of leadership in politics and public service has allowed us to build up a robust institutional memory of dos and don’ts - but amid a global pandemic and the iterative process of change, so many of the IOP’s best characteristics, projects, and initiatives have been lost in the shuffle. We want to bring our better elements back to the forefront and take innovations from the IOP’s past into its future.

  • Career and Student Development
  • Community Embeddedness
  • National Outlook
Career and Student Development
  • Research at HKS - Consistently available and paid RA positions with HKS centers, faculty, and professors offered during term-time.
  • The IOP Research Hub - Creating a repository of available opportunities with professors, HKS Centers, and other projects and lower barriers to access research at HKS through the IOP.
  • Career Readiness - Expanding career readiness, skills workshops, and mentorship programming during J-Term. Additionally, developing a series of mini-lectures on careers in politics with leaders currently working in speechwriting, policymaking, campaign management, political communications, and more.
  • Alumni & Networking - Collaborations on Alumni Networking with the University Alumni Association, including networking nights, match programs, application and resume review opportunities, and more.
  • Jobs & Recruiting - Campaigns and careers job fair for IOP students, with campaign connections and political employers from across the political spectrum and industry
Community Embeddedness
  • Capstone Service Projects - Effective capstone service projects with community organizations and referrals to PBHA service opportunities to elevate the work of the broader campus community.
  • Fellowships & Scholarship - Restore the John C. Culver Scholarship, a fully funded scholarship for IOP students to pursue an MPP or MPA at the Harvard Kennedy School. As recently as 2015, this program existed as a partnership between the IOP and HKS.
  • Opening Our Doors - IOP programming available to the general public and Non-Harvard members of our community, including Forums, open speaker gatherings, service challenges, and more.
Our National Outlook
  • The National Campaign Conference - Engaging college students and young leaders from around the country on the IOP’s grounds with an in-person conference about political issues, civic engagement, and communtiy action.
  • The National Voter Guide - Leveraging the strength of our civic engagement programming to make a national impact by providing voter guides for students across the country from local elections to statewide races.
  • Gov 3.0 - It’s time the IOP renews this programming and signal our return to leadership in civic technology. The IOP had led the country in civic innovation through its Gov 2.0 program, funding notable organizations like UpSolve and the Civic Digital Fellowship.

Revive - Building on What We Have

A quick skim through our IOP Announce newsletter or scroll through our website calendar and you’ll know that the IOP does a lot. Ranging from workshops to speaker sessions to study groups to socials, there is a wealth of public service and politics programming on the IOP’s plate. But an expansive existing agenda means there’s even greater room for growth. We hope to make the IOP’s ongoing operations flow more smoothly, enrich our current programming, and improve on what we do both well and poorly.

  • Administration and Logistics
  • IOP Mobility & Accessibility
  • Programming Diversity
  • Parties and Inclusive Social Spaces
Administration & Logistics
  • A Real Common App - Redisgn the IOP Common App to reduce use time, encourage a wider spread of program membership and lowers barriers to program entry. Guarantee a spot at the IOP using our programming ranking system.
  • Your IOP - Consolidate IOP event registrations and upcoming programming to allow students to track the status and timeline of their applications, RSVPs, and attendance.
  • Staff-Student Connection - Coffee chats with staff on the IOP to learn more about the behind-the-scenes of IOP programming, how to get your projects of the ground.
  • Open Ideation - Monthly staff-student open ideation meetings (featuring snazzy HKS food, of course) or staff to update students on the state of the IOP
  • Sharing Our Work - Publication and release of the IOP’s semesterly "wrap-up" to the entire IOP community, with an emphasis on member spotlights to recognize student work and program achievement.
IOP Mobility and Accessibility
  • Expanding the OneFund - Grow the IOP OneFund to include funds for coffee chats, networking, programming international relations, study abroad, and global research opportunities.
  • Focus Groups - Monthly focus groups on where funding is lacking and what support is needed and working with a staff financial facilitator to ensure a timely response to focus group findings.
  • J-Term & Study Abroad - Strengthening the IOP's international reach by funding J-term study abroad programs to Institute of Government and higher learning around the world.
  • FGLI Inclusion - While we do not identify as members of the FGLI community at Harvard, we recognize the many financial barriers to accessibility at the IOP. After speaking with FGLI students and community leaders, we are committed to bringing back the IOP’s full-ride MPP/MPA awards like the John C. Culver scholarship, continuing our fight for compensating student leaders, and expanding funding for research, J-term and summer opportunities, professional development, campaign fellowships, and term time internships. Additionally, we will be piloting programming specific to FGLI visibility by organizing forums with FGLI indentifying alums and leaders in politics and hosting receptions during opening days and Visitas to discuss financial accessibility at the IOP.
  • Field Trips - Build out IOP Field Trips with an accessible and consistent calendar of outings into Boston, greater Massachusetts, and the nation as a whole, with opportunities for students to propose destinations.
  • IOP Collaboration - There are over 20 recognized Institute of Politics around the country and an even greater number of centers specifically focused on undergraduate political engagement. We will pilot increased of collaborations with nationwide IOPs at Georgetown, UChicago, UPitt, UNC, and beyond for efforts like national civic engagement, advocacy, and political education.
  • Summer of Politics - Every summer, the IOP is everywhere! We will expand Summer in Washington programming to the whole nation highlighting student leaders and supporting their summer experiences with speaker events, paid dinners, and socials from East Coast to West Coast.
  • A Space for Everyone - Streamline entry into HKS on Forum or high-traffic days through notifications to IOP members on high-traffic or Forum, providing members with alternate routes and roombook information well in advance of scheduled programming.
Programming Diversity
  • Diveristy & Outreach Council - Inaugurate a Diversity and Outreach Council to expand on the work of the current Director of Diversity and Outreach, including heritage programming spotlighting student expertise (e.g., a student keynote speaker, student-led teach-ins, or social media takeovers)
  • Sustained Heritage Programming - Heritage months should not be one off opportunities for Affinity based programming. We will push for consistent IOP-wide events on affinity and identity-markers seperate from celebratory months.
  • New Politics - Build historically under-covered disciplines into our flagship IOP programming, including faith in the Forum, STEM in Study Groups, Arts opportunities in Programs and more.
  • Civic Innovation - Reforming Democracy Hackathon in collaboration with the Harvard Innovation Labs and the Harvard STEM and tech to spread a passion for politics beyond a small set of academic disciplines.
Parties & Inclusive Social Spaces
  • Socials - IOP wide socials in collaboration with community orgs like WHRB, College Events Board, Affinity Groups and more in spaces like Mather JCR, dorm parties, and other spots around campus!
  • Block Parties - Monthly IOP block party in the HKS Courtyard with local artists, food trucks, public service programming, civic engagement opportunities, and drinks! (Maybe even a petting zoo!! -- We want to make these events open to suggestion and improvement by the IOP community)
  • Community Building - Use our newly imagined IOP portal to incentivize dinners and outings with IOP families. IOP tailgates at football games, IOP Sponsored Harvard-Yale Shuttles, and community activities at Basketball games, Regattas, and more
  • Using Our Space - Open student office space and IOP front desk with snacks, drinks, office supplies, including access to free printing on campus!
  • Formal under the Forum - An opportunity for a black-tie gala (with expanded OneFund funding for attire) under the forum lights with drinks, food, program and membership recognition, and more.

Reimagine - Creating Service Anew

Public service involves making the status quo work more efficiently and effectively - but there’s more to it than that. The IOP can only reach its full potential if we get comfortable with redefining public service to include the disciplines, perspectives, and narratives that aren’t showcased in the mainstream. Our goal is to revolutionize IOP programming by bringing it into the future of politics and policy and allowing it to leverage the strength of the IOP’s diversity and innovation.

  • Culture of Learning and Living
  • Student Mentorship + Leadership
  • Public Service in Action
  • Your Step-by-Step Experience
Culture of Learning and Living
  • Gender & Sexuality - Launch Gender and Sexuality Coalition(s) to better incorporate LGBTQ+ and women’s issues into each of the IOP’s programs. BGLTQ at the IOP Reception during Opening Days. Annual In-person Pride Month parade gatherings over the summer around the country, led by city-by-city student liaisons.
  • Faith - Collaborate on advocacy and communications initiatives with political/social action chairs at various faith groups on campus. Workshops and special programming hosted by campus chaplains and religious leaders. Policy and advocacy collaborations with Memorial Church, especially on climate change and environmental justice. Designated times and days for interfaith reflections on current events and policy. Religous dinners and receptions for Iftaar, Diwali, Rosh Hashanah, and more (event planning open to student and faith leaders)
  • Academics - Closing the gap between IOP and HKS by introducing Micromasters programs for students to gain accreditation for their academic study in IR, public service, political science, and governance. Faculty open houseses in collaboration with Centers at HKS and across the University. Reading groups for students and open work space in the IOP for students to collaborate. IOP Thesis Prize for outstanding research in public service and leadership. Workshops for graduate fellowships including Trumann, Rhodes, Schwarzman, Mitchell, Fulbright, and more.
  • STEM - Invite STEM faculty to the IOP for Office Hours (transition disciplines each week) to discuss the intersection of their work with public policy. Student Study Networks of IOP students taking pset courses to encourage interdisciplinary study and STEM-student membership. Science and Technology Fellowship program housed under our Directors of Special Projects.
  • Art - Standing galleries of student artwork and creations in the IOP as well as periodic exhibitions around HKS and in the Forum. Launch IOP Creates, a series of hands-on intimate workshops lead by public servant artists on how to create your own and emulate their craft. Publishing arts deliverables of all kinds - an IOP Sketchbook, Poetry Anthology, Album. Workshops on photography and videography in a political context lead by our incredible IOP and HKS staff photographers Arts Fellows housed under our Directors of Special Projects.
  • Mental Health - Pathways to stepping back or stepping into responsibilities throughout the semester (e.g., stepping down to 50%, with set benchmarks for what you can take in or remove). Hours reporting for student leaders and members to give students an idea of how much they’re working and how they can prioritize their workload. Care packages and student support networks so people have an institutional place to turn when things get challenging with IOP related programming.
Student Mentorship + Leadership
  • Highlight Student Leaders - Showcasing student achievements, with awards programs for IOP Families, Seniors, etc. Streamline pathways for students to initiate IOP projects, including creating their own affinity networks and coalitions within the IOP, hosting conferences, workshops, innovation challenges, and more, through a consolidated form for weekly review.
  • Civic Engagement - Campus civic engagement challenges, university wide surveys, and support for administrative policy advocacy
  • Fighting Exlclusivity - Much of the IOP's current programming is only offered to a select group of students either in leadership or on SAC. We will remove exclusive IOP spaces, with more opportunities to engage in SAC-only opportunities, forum moderation, fellows events, etc.
  • IOP Sponsored Alumni Mixers - Working with Harvard Clubs across the country to offer mixers and programming for cities around the nation to offer pathways and networking opportunities in public service and government.
Public Service in Action
  • PBHA Collaborations - Don’t reinvent the wheel with community service - instead, invigorate PBHA collaborations with the IOP, including biweekly steering committee meetings for IOP x PBHA student interfacing, and spotlight their work while steering students to community service.
  • Thematic Service Opportunities - Monthly IOP-wide service outing opportunties based on topics of forums, series of forums, or heritage month.
Your Step-by-Step Experience
  • IOP Portal - IOPortal with personalized student profiles to track registration for events, application statuses, alumni network outreach, staff and student leader communications, and more. The portal will have a built-in alumni directory, referral network, and mock interviews.It will also serve as an all-inclusive student resources, including branding guides, program overviews, and more.
  • G-Suite - IOP-wide shared Google Drive and @iop.harvard.edu emails for student programs
  • Finances - IOP-wide Expensify, biannual reports, and budget tracking to improve financial transparency across the IOP and give students access to the resources they need and ensure timely reimbursement and financial administration.
  • Open SAC Meetings - Open participation at select SAC meetings to lower the barrier between the IOP Student Advisory Committee and general IOP membership.
